<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Mechanics: The Flow and Energy Systems">

What separates this title from other licensed games is its mechanical depth. The combat is built on the **Flow Mechanic** and **Energy Points**. Flow is not just a movement tool; it is a universal system that allows every character to cancel animations into evasive dashes or slips.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Mastering Flow">
    Flow allows you to slip under projectiles or launch into the air with a single input. Learning the specific Flow routes for your main is the first step to high-level play.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Building Energy">
    Energy Points (EP) are gained through successful attacks and well-timed blocks. You can store up to three bars of Energy to power your most devastating moves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enhanced Special Moves">
    By spending one bar of Energy, you can enhance a special move (e.g., Zuko’s fire spin), adding armor, extra hits, or better recovery frames.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Cinematic Super Arts">
    The ultimate expression of bending. Spending all three bars triggers a cinematic Super Art that deals massive damage and can turn the tide of a losing round.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Meter Management">
Do not waste your Energy on enhanced specials if they won't secure a knockdown. In the current 2026 meta, saving meter for a Super Art "punish" is often more effective than small damage boosts.
</Callout>

The bending styles are also deeply integrated into the frame data. Earthbenders generally have slower start-up frames but higher "super armor" properties, while Airbenders have the fastest recovery frames in the game, making them incredibly difficult to punish on block.

</Section>
